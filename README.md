# 👥 HR Analytics Dashboard

### 📖 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee data to understand workforce dynamics, attrition patterns, and factors influencing employee retention within an organization.
The analysis follows a structured data analytics workflow — starting from raw employee datasets, performing data cleaning and preparation, analyzing workforce metrics, and finally visualizing insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard.

The objective of this project is to transform raw HR data into meaningful insights that help organizations understand employee behavior, identify attrition drivers, and support better workforce management decisions.

### 🎯 Purpose of the Analysis
The main goals of this analysis are to:
- Understand employee attrition patterns within the organization
- Identify factors influencing employee turnover
- Analyze how demographics, job roles, and work conditions affect retention
- Evaluate employee distribution across departments and roles
- Provide HR teams with actionable insights through interactive dashboards

### 📊 Key Metrics Analyzed
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate
- Active Employees
- Average Age of Employees
- Attrition distribution by department
- Attrition by job role
- Attrition by education field
- Attrition by age group
- Attrition by salary slab

### 🗂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains employee-level information related to demographics, job roles, income, satisfaction levels, and work experience.
Key data categories include:
- Employee demographics (Age, Gender, Marital Status)
- Job-related details (Department, Job Role, Job Level)
- Compensation information (Monthly Income, Salary Slab, Salary Hike)
- Work experience (Total Working Years, Years at Company, Years in Current Role)
- Work environment metrics (Job Satisfaction, Environment Satisfaction, Work-Life Balance)
- Attrition status of employees
The dataset was cleaned and structured before building the dashboard to ensure accurate analysis and visualization.

### 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used
- Excel / CSV – Dataset storage and initial inspection
- Power BI – Data modeling and interactive dashboard creation
- Power Query – Data transformation and preparation
- DAX – Calculated measures and KPIs
- GitHub – Project documentation and version control

### 🔎 Business Questions Answered
- This analysis answers several important HR business questions:
- What is the overall employee attrition rate?
- Which departments experience the highest attrition?
- Which job roles have the highest employee turnover?
- How does attrition vary across different age groups?
- Which salary slabs experience the most attrition?
- How does education background influence attrition?
- What is the distribution of employees across departments?
- What demographic segments show higher risk of leaving the company?

### 📌 Key Takeaways
- Certain job roles experience significantly higher attrition rates
- Mid-level salary groups show higher employee turnover
- Younger age groups tend to have slightly higher attrition compared to senior employees
- Attrition patterns vary across departments and education fields
- Workforce insights can help HR teams design better employee retention strategies
